Where the model disagrees with the market 💸

The market (bookmakers & Polymarket) is the toughest "rival model" out there. When our model gives an outcome a higher chance than the market's no-vig price implies, that's an edge — a possible value bet. EV (expected value) is your average profit per 1 unit staked if the model is right. green = value · red = market disagrees

🧠 Reality check: a huge edge usually means the model is wrong, not that you found free money — deep, liquid markets (Polymarket has tens of millions in volume) are hard to beat. Our sim tends to over-rate favourites for the title because it doesn't fully capture tournament chaos. Treat big outright edges as "the model is bullish here," not as a lock.

How much can you trust it? 🧪

We backtested the model on real past tournaments and scored it against simpler rivals. Log loss is the headline number: it punishes confident-but-wrong predictions — lower is better. A model that just guessed randomly scores ~1.10; beating that, and beating the simpler football models, is the whole game.

✅ Strengths

  • Beats Elo-only, recent-form, and Dixon-Coles baselines on backtest.
  • Uses expected goals (shot quality), not just results.
  • Squad & lineup layer: rates the projected XI + keeper, so strong-roster teams (e.g. France) aren't under-rated by national Elo alone.
  • Adjusts for injuries / availability of expected starters.
  • Head-to-head history adds a small but real, validated edge.

⚠️ Weaknesses

  • Football is low-scoring & chaotic — single games are hard.
  • Tends to over-rate South American sides for the title vs the market.
  • Coach quality isn't modelled yet (no leak-free historical data).
  • Neutral-venue World Cup is unusual vs the training data.
